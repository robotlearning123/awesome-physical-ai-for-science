# Contributing to Awesome Physical AI for Science

Thank you for your interest in contributing!

## Guidelines

1. **Relevance**: Resources should be related to Physical AI for Science — the intersection of robotics/automation with AI-driven scientific discovery.

2. **Quality**: Only include well-documented, actively maintained, or historically significant resources.

3. **Format**: Follow the existing table format:
   ```markdown
   | [Name](URL) | Description | Year/Stars |
   ```

4. **Categories**: Place resources in the most specific applicable category.

5. **No duplicates**: Check existing entries before adding.

6. **PR Process**:
   - Fork the repository
   - Add your resource in the appropriate section
   - Submit a Pull Request with a brief description

## What to contribute

- ✅ Self-driving lab systems and papers
- ✅ Lab automation frameworks and tools
- ✅ AI agent systems for scientific workflows
- ✅ Robotic experimentation platforms
- ✅ Relevant companies and startups
- ✅ Benchmarks and datasets
- ✅ Surveys and review papers

## What NOT to contribute

- ❌ General AI/ML resources (use awesome-ai-for-science instead)
- ❌ Pure software-only AI tools without physical/lab component
- ❌ Commercial products without public documentation
- ❌ Self-promotion without substance
